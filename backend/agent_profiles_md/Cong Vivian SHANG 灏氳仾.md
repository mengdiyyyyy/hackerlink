### Personal Background

Growing up, I’ve been fortunate to call many different places home. Born in a small town in Shaanxi, I moved to Beijing at 11. Halfway through high school, I transferred to a boarding school in the suburbs of Connecticut and began my decade-long journey in the United States. I spent much of my 20s in New York City, where I completed college, graduate school, and started my professional career. In 2021, an opportunity to work at the Chinese Academy of Sciences brought me to Shanghai, so I moved yet again. Now, my husband and I, along with our two cats, consider Shanghai our home with tremendous joy.

### Career Progress to Date

I started my career in New York City as a program associate at a start-up incubator. A somewhat drastic departure from my clinical training as a Dietitian, the start-up world quickly taught me how the business side operated, filling the gap in my technical training. During that time, I had the chance to meet professionals from various disciplines, notably the founders of Season, a nutrition tele-health start-up. This instant connection led me to join Season as its first employee. To date, the company has secured $36.6 million in funding, and I take great pride in helping build the product from day one to launch. In 2021, an opportunity to work on the commercialization of nutrition research at the Chinese Academy of Sciences brought me to Shanghai. I led several commercialization projects and co-authored a paper published in The British Medical Journal.

In 2022, I redirected my focus to address the increasing demands of our family business. I joined the wealth management branch of our business with the goal to build the scaffolding of a family office.
