### Personal Background

I am from a picturesque village in Kerala, India. Known for its natural backwaters, beaches lined up with coconut trees and the warm and welcoming people, the environment I grew up had a long-lasting impact on me as a person. Surrounded by the warmth of my parents and grandparents, I grew up enveloped in a cocoon of joy and steadfast values. The one thing my parents always taught me was to be independent and be responsible for oneself from a very young age. I earned my Bachelor of Science in Mathematics from Sacred Heart College, Mahatma Gandhi University, Kerala. I went to a different city, Chennai in India for my first job as an Analyst in Royal Bank of Scotland. I started loving the 12-hour train journeys from my work place to my home town. Even though we used to travel earlier as a family, these solo journeys instilled in me a need to explore more places and cultures. And hence I love travelling, trying the local cuisines, interacting with people and falling in love with the diversity the earth offers.

### Career Progress to Date

I began my career as a Process Associate in The Royal Bank of Scotland, later NatWest Bank. I was into a team handling post payment investigation for the bank's customers in several European countries. The first year in my career paved a strong foundation by learning about SWIFT, International banking and clearing mechanisms. As I started climbing the career ladder and took leadership roles, I upskilled myself to the changing trends in banking, especially in international payments through various certifications. I migrated a critical team from Manchester, UK to India. This was a big leap in my career and a milestone in my life where I decided that I want to pursue my career with an organization that contributes to the society and give better outcomes for customers through sustainable products. Later, I managed a 15-member team handling post payment investigation and compensation activities. I decided to take a break to explore new places and spend time in introspection. Refreshed and upskilled after eight months, I transitioned to Wells Fargo, delving into data analytics as an Analytics consultant. How ever the role was short lived as a family relocation to Beijing made me take another unintended career break.
