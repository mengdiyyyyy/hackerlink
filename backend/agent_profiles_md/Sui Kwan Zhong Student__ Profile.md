### Personal Background

I was born in Hong Kong, and when I was 10 years old, my parents emigrated to the United States. Since then, I’ve grown up in the vibrant San Francisco Bay Area. For my undergraduate studies, I was fortunate to receive a full-ride scholarship to UC Berkeley, where I majored in Legal Studies with the aspiration of becoming a lawyer. Many of my classmates went on to work at corporate law firms after graduation, and with my degree and honors, I could have easily followed the same path. However, I felt a strong desire to serve a greater purpose and make a positive impact on society.

My first role after graduation was with the law department at Swords to Plowshares, a nonprofit organization dedicated to serving military veterans. Around the same time, I developed an interest in my family’s restaurant food-packaging business. Our company’s supply chain includes over thirty factories, primarily in East Asia. Eager to contribute to the family business’s growth, I took on the role of Vice President of Purchasing and relocated to Shanghai.

### Career Progress to Date

In my role as Vice President of Purchasing for my family’s restaurant food-packaging business, I have been instrumental in driving revenue growth, introducing innovative product designs, and managing our supplier relationships. Our client list in the U.S. includes major companies such as Costco, Restaurant Depot, and Smart & Final. Recently, our pizza boxes were featured in an article in the L.A. Times, with a photo of Governor Gavin Newsom and Vice President Kamala Harris holding our products.

Additionally, I co-founded a series of beverage stores in Shanghai with a business partner. By 2022, our product line had achieved the highest sales volume in two regions of Shanghai on various takeout platforms. Despite my years of managerial experience, I recognize the need to deepen my knowledge in areas such as accounting, finance, marketing, and economics, which are essential for the continued expansion of my small business.

### Career goals

My long-term goal is to expand our family business both internationally and within the current industry by introducing new products and diversifying into related sectors. I am particularly focused on two emerging trends: the increasing emphasis on ecological sustainability and the integration of advanced technology. As global wealth increases, so does the demand for a higher quality of life, including a clean environment. I believe technology can be harnessed not just for abundance but for delivering quality and sustainability. A key example of this is the development of biodegradable or even edible food packaging. Navigating the intersection of ecological consciousness and technological innovation will be a central challenge in my career as I continue to develop our family’s food packaging business.

I am also passionate about entrepreneurship and aspire to start another company in the future. I’m open to exploring various fields and hope to learn from my professors and peers to identify new entrepreneurial opportunities and trends.

### Reason(s) for doing an MBA at Tsinghua in China now

I believe that the Tsinghua Global MBA program will equip me with the skills and knowledge needed to lead our family business effectively and to succeed as an entrepreneur. The program will broaden my understanding of all aspects of business beyond purchasing, including sales, operations, and marketing. I plan to apply these skills to modernize our marketing strategies, grow our customer base, and prepare for my next entrepreneurial venture. Additionally, I am eager to immerse myself in Chinese culture and gain insights into China’s business landscape and how to collaborate with Chinese professionals.

### The most important thing you’ve learned (or known) about China

I have a deep appreciation for Chinese culture, particularly its rich history, traditional music, dance, art, opera, and fashion. I’m an avid listener of opera from around the world, and I’ve learned to appreciate traditional Chinese opera which is called "Xiqu." There are various forms of Xiqu in China, with the most famous being Peking Opera, or “Jingju.” Other notable styles include Cantonese Opera, Yue Opera, and Huangmei Opera, each with its distinct style, regional influences, and historical background. So far, I’ve had the pleasure of experiencing Peking Opera, Yue Opera, and Huangmei Opera, and I look forward to discovering more in the future.
